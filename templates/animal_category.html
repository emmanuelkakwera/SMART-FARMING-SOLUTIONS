Common Diseases & Treatments

{% if category == 'chickens' %}

Newcastle Disease High Risk

Symptoms:

  • Respiratory distress and gasping
  • Greenish diarrhea
  • Twisted neck and paralysis
  • Sudden drop in egg production
  • High mortality rate (up to 90%)

Treatment Protocol:

  • No specific treatment - focus on prevention
  • Vaccinate with Lasota strain at 2 weeks
  • Isolate and cull affected birds
  • Disinfect housing thoroughly
  • Provide supportive care with vitamins

Prevention: Regular vaccination, biosecurity, quarantine new birds

Fowl Pox Medium Risk

Symptoms:

  • Wart-like lesions on comb and wattles
  • Yellow cankers in mouth and throat
  • Difficulty breathing and eating
  • Reduced egg production
  • Weight loss and lethargy

Treatment Protocol:

  • Apply iodine to skin lesions
  • Remove mouth lesions carefully
  • Provide soft, moist feed
  • Antibiotics for secondary infections
  • Vitamin supplements for recovery

Prevention: Vaccination at 8 weeks, mosquito control, clean housing

Coccidiosis High Risk

Symptoms:

  • Bloody or mucoid diarrhea
  • Pale comb and wattles
  • Ruffled feathers and huddling
  • Loss of appetite and weight
  • Sudden death in young chicks

Treatment Protocol:

  • Amprolium in drinking water for 5-7 days
  • Sulfa drugs for severe cases
  • Electrolytes to prevent dehydration
  • Probiotics to restore gut flora
  • Clean and dry litter management

Prevention: Good sanitation, coccidiostats in feed, dry litter

Management Practices

Housing Requirements

Space: 2-3 square feet per bird

Ventilation: Good air circulation essential

Lighting: 14-16 hours light for layers

Nesting: 1 nest box per 4-5 hens

Feeding Guidelines

Starter: 18-20% protein (0-8 weeks)

Grower: 16-18% protein (8-18 weeks)

Layer: 16-18% protein + calcium

Water: Fresh water available always

Health Monitoring

Daily: Check feed/water consumption

Weekly: Monitor weight gain

Monthly: Deworming schedule

Quarterly: Vaccination boosters
